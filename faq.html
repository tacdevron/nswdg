Career Paths

On this page, we will be explaining the different branches, roles and pipelines we provide.

United States Navy

Navy SEALs are highly trained special operations forces involved in direct action warfare, special reconnaissance, counter-terrorism, and unconventional warfare. Every role within our Navy department has to go through an application phase, interview phase, and S&T (Green Team) phase. Upon successful completion of S&T, you will be given full access to the main server. You may choose what team and role you want, but there is no guarantee of selection.

EOD Technicians are responsible for safely disposing of explosives and hazardous materials, ensuring the safety of military personnel and civilians. Every role within our Navy department has to go through an application phase, interview phase, and S&T (Green Team) phase. Upon successful completion of S&T, you will be given full access to the main server. You may choose what team and role you want, but there is no guarantee of selection.

Special Warfare Boat Operators operate small boats in support of special operations missions, particularly in maritime environments. Every role within our Navy department has to go through an application phase, interview phase, and S&T (Green Team) phase. Upon successful completion of S&T, you will be given full access to the main server. You may choose what team and role you want, but there is no guarantee of selection.

United States Air Force, Army & CIA

Combat Controllers are highly trained special operators responsible for providing command and control of air assets to ground forces during missions. They undergo rigorous selection and training, including an application phase, interview phase, and Special Tactics (ST) phase. Successful completion grants access to specialized roles, though assignment is competitive and not guaranteed.

Pararescuemen (PJs) are elite special operations forces trained to rescue and provide medical treatment in hostile environments. Their missions include personnel recovery, combat search and rescue (CSAR), and humanitarian assistance. Similar to Combat Control, PJs go through a rigorous selection process involving application, interviews, and completion of Special Tactics (ST) training. Successful candidates gain access to specialized roles, though assignments are competitive.

Rotary Wing Aviators are pilots specialized in rotary-wing aircraft, such as helicopters. They are essential for various missions including transport, reconnaissance, and combat support. Joining requires extensive training and selection, including flight school and operational assignments.

Paramilitary Operations Officers are highly trained professionals who conduct paramilitary and intelligence operations in support of national security objectives. They undergo rigorous training in intelligence collection, operational planning, and leadership. Selection is competitive and demanding, with assignments focused on critical missions worldwide.
